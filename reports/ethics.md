# Ethics and responsible use

EvidRoute is designed to make uncertainty, evidence provenance, source conflicts, privacy
denials, and abstention visible. Those controls can reduce some failure modes but do not make the
system inherently truthful or safe.

Potential harms include confident use of incorrect source material, leakage through poorly
configured external routes, benchmark overfitting, automation bias, selective reporting of
metrics, and misleading claims of institutional affiliation or research validation. Mitigations
include verified-mode citations, local-only defaults, typed failures, trace export, explicit
claim boundaries, and no fabricated or backdated results.

Users remain responsible for source rights, privacy, security review, domain validation, and
human oversight.
